Best Ecommerce Platform for D2C Brands in India (2025)

By MellowKraft  ·  March 2025  ·  10 min read

There is no single best ecommerce platform. There's only the right one for where your brand is today — and the wrong one to stay on after you've outgrown it.

This guide cuts through the noise. We'll walk through every major option available to Indian D2C brands in 2025, who each one is designed for, what it costs at scale, and when you should move on.

The Quick Answer (by GMV stage)

Monthly GMV Recommended Platform Why
Under ₹5L Shopify Basic or Dukaan Low cost, fast setup, no engineering needed
₹5L – ₹25L Shopify (standard plan) Best ecosystem at this scale, acceptable fees
₹25L – ₹75L Shopify or headless Shopify Costs growing — evaluate custom frontend
₹75L+ Custom commerce stack Transaction fees alone justify ownership

Platform-by-Platform Breakdown

Use it: ₹0–₹50L/mo

Shopify

The global standard for a reason. Shopify gives you a fully functional store out of the box — themes, checkout, payment gateway integrations (Razorpay, PayU work well), shipping, analytics, and an enormous app ecosystem.

What it costs in India: Basic at ~₹2,000/month, Shopify at ~₹5,500, Advanced at ~₹22,000. Add 0.5–2% transaction fees on every order (Shopify Payments is not available in India), plus ₹10,000–₹50,000/month in apps as you scale.

The ceiling: At ₹50L+ GMV, transaction fees alone eat ₹25,000–₹1,00,000/month. Checkout customisation is limited. Your OMS is a patchwork of apps. Multi-warehouse inventory requires workarounds. These aren't bugs — they're deliberate limits imposed by the platform architecture.

Verdict: Best platform under ₹50L/month. Gets expensive and constrained above it.

Consider it: ₹0–₹10L/mo, tech-savvy teams

WooCommerce

WooCommerce is a WordPress plugin — it's free to install, but the total cost of ownership is higher than most brands realise. You need to manage your own hosting, security patches, plugin updates, and performance optimisation. For a technical founder comfortable with WordPress, it works. For everyone else, it's a maintenance burden.

What it costs: "Free" plugin, but hosting costs ₹3,000–₹15,000/month for decent performance at scale. Premium plugins for subscriptions, multi-currency, advanced shipping add ₹5,000–₹20,000/month. Developer time to keep it running: significant.

The ceiling: Performance degrades at scale without expensive infrastructure. Not built for high-volume D2C operations. Checkout customisation requires custom development. No SaaS-style reliability guarantees.

Verdict: Good for low-budget launches with a technical team. Outgrown quickly. Not suitable above ₹10–15L/month without significant engineering investment.

Use it: Early stage ₹0–₹5L/mo

Dukaan / Instamojo

India-native platforms designed for first-time sellers — quick setup, Hindi-language support, COD support built-in, WhatsApp integrations. Excellent for validation and early sales before you've found product-market fit.

What it costs: ₹500–₹3,000/month depending on plan. Limited apps and integrations.

The ceiling: Limited customisation, small developer ecosystem, no path to headless or custom architecture. Hit the ceiling fast. Good for testing, not for scaling.

Verdict: Perfect for the first ₹50L in cumulative revenue. Migrate when you see consistent monthly GMV above ₹3–5L.

Consider it: ₹25L–₹75L/mo, design-first brands

Shopify + Custom Headless Frontend

You keep Shopify as your backend (product catalog, orders, checkout) and build a fully custom React or Next.js storefront that talks to Shopify via their Storefront API. You get complete design freedom and custom pre-checkout logic, while Shopify still handles payments and order processing.

What it costs: ₹8–20 lakh to build the custom frontend. Shopify subscription continues. Transaction fees continue. Development agency or in-house engineer required to maintain the frontend.

The ceiling: Still paying Shopify transaction fees. Still dependent on Shopify's backend roadmap. Still limited by Shopify's checkout customisation at the actual payment step. Good mid-step, not a final destination.

Verdict: Right move for design-forward brands at ₹25–75L who need UX freedom but aren't ready to migrate the backend.

Best above ₹75L/mo

Custom Commerce Stack (MellowKraft)

A fully custom platform where you own everything: the storefront, the checkout, the order management system, the inventory system, the CRM, and the marketing layer. No platform dependency. No transaction tax. No app fragmentation.

What it costs: ₹15–40 lakh to build. Infrastructure (hosting, CDN, databases) costs ₹10,000–₹40,000/month — but you've eliminated Shopify's transaction fees and app stack costs, which often exceeds this.

The ceiling: You are the ceiling. Any feature you need can be built. Any integration is possible. Your architecture scales with your business instead of constraining it.

Verdict: The right answer for any brand doing ₹75L+ GMV per month, or any brand with complex operations (multi-warehouse, subscriptions, D2C + offline) that Shopify can't serve cleanly.

The Hidden Costs Nobody Talks About

When comparing platforms, most guides compare subscription costs. This misses the full picture. Here's what to actually measure:

  • Transaction fees: Shopify's percentage cut on every order — the biggest hidden cost at scale
  • App costs: Advanced features always require paid apps on Shopify and WooCommerce
  • Developer time: Maintaining workarounds and integrations costs engineering hours
  • Opportunity cost: Features you can't build on your current platform = revenue you can't unlock
  • Migration cost (future): The longer you stay on the wrong platform, the more expensive the eventual migration

At ₹1 crore/month GMV, the real cost of Shopify (transaction fees + apps + subscription) is ₹1.5–3 lakh per month. A custom platform typically costs ₹30,000–₹50,000/month in infrastructure. The ₹1–2.5 lakh monthly gap pays for the build cost in under 18 months.

What Indian D2C Brands Specifically Need

Most platform guides are written for Western brands. Indian D2C has specific requirements that change the calculus:

  • COD (Cash on Delivery): Still 40–60% of orders for most categories. Your platform and OMS need to handle COD returns, reconciliation, and partial payments natively.
  • WhatsApp commerce: Selling through WhatsApp is increasingly important. Your platform needs clean API access to integrate with WhatsApp Business properly.
  • Shiprocket / Delhivery / Ecom Express: Indian logistics providers have their own APIs. Shopify apps exist, but they're mediocre. A custom integration is far more reliable.
  • Razorpay / PayU: Native integration without Shopify's transaction fee overhead saves significant margin at scale.
  • GST compliance: Invoicing, HSN codes, multi-state GST — these need to be built into your OMS, not bolted on.

None of these are impossible on Shopify, but all of them are significantly better handled on a platform built specifically for Indian D2C operations.

Our Recommendation

If you're under ₹25L/month: stay on Shopify and focus entirely on growing revenue. The platform constraint is not your bottleneck yet.

If you're between ₹25L–₹75L/month: evaluate your app stack costs, transaction fee run rate, and where your ops team spends manual effort. These three numbers will tell you if it's time to move.

If you're above ₹75L/month: the math already favours owning your stack. The question is no longer whether, but when and how to migrate without disruption.
