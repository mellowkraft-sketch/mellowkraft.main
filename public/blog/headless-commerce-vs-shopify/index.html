Headless Commerce vs Shopify: Which One Is Right for Your Brand?

By MellowKraft  ·  March 2025  ·  8 min read

Every founder who's hit a ceiling on Shopify eventually hears the words "go headless." And then spends three hours in a rabbit hole trying to understand what headless actually means, whether they need it, and if there's an easier option.

This article gives you the honest version — when Shopify is the right answer, when it's not, and what "headless" and "custom" actually mean for an Indian D2C brand.

What "Headless Commerce" Actually Means

Traditional ecommerce platforms like Shopify bundle the frontend (what customers see) with the backend (cart, checkout, inventory, orders) in one tightly coupled system. You pick a theme, the theme talks to Shopify's backend, everything works together but on Shopify's terms.

Headless separates these two layers. The backend — product catalog, cart, checkout, order management — runs independently via APIs. The frontend is a custom-built React, Next.js, or native mobile app that talks to that backend. You control both how it looks and how it behaves.

Headless doesn't mean "no Shopify." Many brands go headless while keeping Shopify as the backend. They build a custom storefront, but Shopify still powers checkout and inventory. The next step — what MellowKraft does — is replacing the backend too.

When Shopify Is Still the Right Call

Let's be honest about this. Shopify is genuinely excellent at a certain scale. If you're in this range, staying on Shopify is the right decision:

  • GMV below ₹20–25 lakh/month
  • Product catalog under 500 SKUs with standard variations
  • No complex fulfillment logic (single warehouse, standard shipping)
  • Standard checkout flow — no subscriptions, bundles, or split payments
  • Team doesn't have dedicated engineering capacity

Shopify at this stage gives you a fully functional store with payment gateway integrations, mobile-ready themes, and solid analytics — without needing to build or maintain infrastructure. The trade-off (the platform fee, the template constraints) is worth it because the alternative (building your own) costs more and requires engineering you don't yet have.

When Shopify Becomes a Ceiling

The problem is that Shopify's design — optimised for quick launch and moderate scale — becomes a structural constraint at higher volumes and complexity:

What Shopify limits

  • Checkout customisation (Shopify Scripts has hard limits)
  • Custom fulfillment logic across multiple warehouses
  • Real-time inventory across offline + online channels
  • Advanced subscription and recurring billing flows
  • Unified customer profile across purchase history + CRM
  • Custom loyalty, referral, and bundle mechanics

What custom/headless unlocks

  • Any checkout flow you can design
  • Multi-warehouse, multi-location inventory logic
  • Real-time unified inventory across all channels
  • Subscription billing built into your own system
  • One customer record across all touchpoints
  • No app dependency — it's all native to your platform

The Three Stages of Outgrowing Shopify

Stage 1: You want a different-looking store

Most brands start here. Their Shopify theme looks like every other brand. The solution at this stage is usually a custom Shopify theme or Shopify 2.0 sections — not going headless. Cost: ₹1–5 lakh. No backend migration needed.

Stage 2: You need non-standard checkout or product logic

This is where headless Shopify (custom frontend, Shopify backend) makes sense. You build your storefront in React or Next.js using Shopify's Storefront API and Checkout API. You get full design freedom and custom pre-checkout logic, while Shopify still handles cart and checkout. Cost: ₹8–20 lakh. Engineering effort: moderate.

Stage 3: The backend itself is the bottleneck

At this stage, Shopify's order management, inventory system, or fee structure itself is the problem. No frontend change helps. You need a fully custom commerce stack — your own OMS, IMS, storefront, and checkout — built and owned by you. This is what MellowKraft builds. Cost: ₹15–40 lakh. ROI typically within 12–24 months at ₹50L+ GMV.

Signals That You're at Stage 3

  • ⚡Transaction fees are meaningfully cutting into your margins — especially painful at ₹50L+/month
  • ⚡You have 6+ Shopify apps and they don't share data cleanly with each other
  • ⚡Your ops team is doing manual work because Shopify's OMS doesn't support your fulfillment model
  • ⚡You want to sell across WhatsApp, offline, and online with a single inventory system
  • ⚡Your engineers spend more time working around Shopify's limitations than building product

What "Fully Custom" vs "Headless Shopify" Looks Like in Practice

To make this concrete: a headless Shopify setup still charges you Shopify's transaction fees on every order. A fully custom platform does not — you integrate directly with Razorpay or PayU at their rates only. For a brand at ₹1 crore/month, this difference is ₹5–20 lakh per year.

Headless Shopify also means you're still dependent on Shopify's backend roadmap. If Shopify changes their API, raises prices, or deprecates a feature, you're affected. With a fully custom backend, you own your infrastructure entirely.

The Bottom Line

Shopify is a great product for the scale it's designed for. The mistake isn't using Shopify — it's staying on it after you've outgrown it. The cost of staying (transaction fees, app fragmentation, architectural constraints) compounds over time, while the cost of migrating gets easier as tooling improves.

If you're at ₹50L+ GMV per month, running complex operations, and feeling constrained — the math has already shifted in favour of owning your stack.
